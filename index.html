About Nile International Academy

Our Educational Philosophy

Founded in 2005, Nile International Academy is a premier bilingual institution in Cairo offering American curriculum education with strong Arabic language and cultural programs. We prepare students for global citizenship while maintaining deep connections to Egyptian heritage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What curriculum do you follow?

We follow an American curriculum aligned with Common Core standards, enhanced with Arabic language and Islamic studies for Muslim students. Our program prepares students for both American and Egyptian university systems.

What are the school hours?

School operates Sunday through Thursday from 7:30 AM to 3:00 PM. Extended care is available until 5:00 PM for working parents. Extra-curricular activities typically run from 3:15 PM to 4:30 PM.

Do you offer transportation?

Yes, we provide safe and monitored transportation services covering most areas of Cairo. Our buses are equipped with GPS tracking, seat belts, and are supervised by trained attendants.

What universities do your graduates attend?

Our graduates have been accepted to top universities worldwide including Harvard, MIT, Stanford, AUC, Cairo University, and universities in the UK, Canada, and throughout Europe.

Academic Calendar

The academic year runs from September to June with three terms. We observe both Egyptian and international holidays. Summer programs are available in July for enrichment and review.

Key dates include parent-teacher conferences in October and March, mid-term breaks in November and April, and final exams in May. Detailed calendar available upon request.

Admissions Process

Admissions are selective and based on previous academic records, entrance assessments, and family interviews. Applications for the upcoming academic year open in January.

Required documents include birth certificate, previous school records, passport photos, and medical reports. Scholarships available for exceptional students.

Student Support

We provide comprehensive support including learning resource center, counseling services, college advising, and special education support. Our student-teacher ratio is 15:1.

Additional services include after-school tutoring, university test preparation (SAT/ACT), and career guidance programs starting from Grade 9.
